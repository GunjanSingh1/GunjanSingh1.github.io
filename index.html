About

I am a Ph. D student at the Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology (IIIT), Delhi and also an IBM Research Fellow. My academic advisors are Dr. Raghava Mutharaju (IIIT, Delhi) and Dr. Sumit Bhatia (Adobe, India).

My doctoral research focused on developing benchmarks for description logic reasoners, encompassing static, streaming, and neuro-symbolic reasoners. This work has given me extensive expertise in several knowledge graphs and semantic web-related technologies, such as Ontology (OWL 2) Modeling, Conventional Static Reasoning (Tableau and Rule-based reasoners), SPARQL Query Engines, Temporal and Stream Reasoning, and Neuro-Symbolic Reasoning (including KG Completion, Deep-Deductive, and Inductive Reasoning methods). I have also worked on projects exploring non-conventional learning-based methods for efficient reasoning over expressive ontology languages such as OWL 2. My research interests include Machine Learning and Reinforcement Learning.

Prior to joining my Ph. D, I worked as an Assistant Professor at Galgotias College of Engineering and Technology (A. K. T. U) for 2.5 years. I completed my Masters in Computer Science and Engineering with two gold-medals from Aligarh Muslim University (A. M. U), India, 2016 and Bachelors in Computer Engineering from Aligarh Muslim University (A. M. U), India, 2014.

Projects

  • OWL2Bench

    A customizable benchmark for OWL 2 reasoners. This project focuses on creating various benchmarking scenarios to evaluate the performance of different reasoners.

  • ConfigOWL2Bench

    An extension of OWL2Bench that allows for dynamic configuration of benchmark parameters to tailor testing environments according to specific requirements.

  • GenACT

    An ontology-based temporal web data generator designed to simulate academic conference tweets, providing data for research in temporal reasoning and knowledge graph evolution.

  • Configurable Ontology Generator

    A tool for generating ontologies with customizable parameters to facilitate testing and development of ontology-based applications.

  • NeSyBench

    A tool for generating ontologies with customizable parameters to facilitate testing and development of ontology-based applications.

  • TLDR

    A project aimed at developing a tool for summarizing large documents into concise and readable abstracts, making it easier for researchers to grasp key information quickly.

  • ARC

    An advanced reasoning component designed to improve the efficiency and accuracy of knowledge graph reasoning tasks through innovative algorithms and techniques.
