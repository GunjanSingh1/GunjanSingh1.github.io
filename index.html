Profile

About

I'm a postdoctoral researcher in the Information Service Engineering group at FIZ Karlsruhe – Leibniz Institute for Information Infrastructure, working with Prof. Dr. Harald Sack. My work centers on ontology reasoning, benchmarking, and neuro-symbolic reasoning — building the tools and datasets needed to evaluate how reasoners handle expressive OWL 2 ontologies, at scale and under change.

I completed my PhD at the Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology (IIIT), Delhi, where I was also an IBM Research Fellow, advised by Dr. Raghava Mutharaju (IIIT Delhi) and Dr. Sumit Bhatia (Adobe Research, India). My doctoral research introduced benchmarks for description logic reasoners spanning static, streaming, and neuro-symbolic settings, giving me a broad grounding in OWL 2 modeling, tableau- and rule-based reasoning, SPARQL query engines, temporal and stream reasoning, and neuro-symbolic methods for knowledge graph completion and deductive/inductive reasoning.

Before my PhD, I spent 2.5 years as an Assistant Professor at Galgotias College of Engineering and Technology (AKTU). I hold a Master's — with two gold medals — and a Bachelor's in Computer Engineering, both from Aligarh Muslim University, India.
